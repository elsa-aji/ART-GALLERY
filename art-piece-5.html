The Scream

by Edvard Munch

Year of Creation: 1893

Category: Expressionism, Psychological

Description

The Scream is an iconic expressionist masterpiece by Edvard Munch, created in 1893. This haunting artwork depicts a figure clutching their face in existential dread, set against a swirling, turbulent landscape. The painting explores themes of anxiety, isolation, and emotional anguish. Munch's bold use of color and distorted forms revolutionized modern art, influencing generations of artists. The Scream remains one of the most recognizable and impactful artworks in human history, symbolizing the modern human condition.

Medium

Oil, tempera, and pastel on cardboard
